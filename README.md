# WEDE5020-POE-
## ORGANISATION OVERVIEW
Name: TD Events by Design
TD Events by Design is a small event planning business based in AcornHoek, Bushbuckridge. The company specialises in organising and managing a wide range of events, providing professional and creative services tailored to each client’s needs. The business was established to offer affordable and high-quality event solutions within the local community and surrounding areas.

## SERVICES OFFERED
•	Funeral arrangements
•	VIP setups
•	Catering services
•	Weddings
•	Transportation
•	Kiddies parties
•	Baby showers
•	Bridal showers

## MISSION
To provide reliable, creative, and high-quality event planning services that exceed customer expectations.

## VISION
To become a trusted and leading event planning brand in Bushbuckridge and across Mpumalanga.

## WEBSITE GOALS AND OBJECTIVES
The main goal of the website is to establish a strong online presence for TD Events by Design and make it easier for customers to access information and services.

## CURRENT WEBSITE ANALYSIS
TD Events by Design currently does not have an official website. This limits its ability to reach a wider audience and attract new clients outside the local community. Customers mainly rely on word-of-mouth and social media platforms for information.

Weaknesses:
•	No formal online presence
•	Limited visibility and marketing reach
•	No central platform for service information
Areas for Improvement:
•	Develop a professional website
•	Showcase services and past work
•	Provide an easy way for customers to make enquiries and bookings


## PROPOSED WEBSITE FEATURES AND FUNCTIONALITY
The website will include the following key pages and features:
•	Homepage: Introduction and overview of services
•	About Us: Background, mission, and vision
•	Services: Detailed list of services offered
•	Products: Images of past events
•	Enquiry Page: Form for booking and service requests
•	Contact Page: Contact details and location
The website will allow users to easily navigate between pages and submit enquiries.
